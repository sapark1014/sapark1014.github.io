### Overview

[ImageInLife](https://www.imageinlife.eu/) was a European training network dedicated to developing advanced image analysis methods for biological and biomedical applications. The consortium brought together researchers across mathematics, computer science, and biology to create computational tools for analyzing complex microscopy data of living systems.

### My Contribution

I developed a novel mathematical and computational approach for **segmentation-based cell tracking** in fluorescence microscopy videos of macrophages. The method addresses the challenge of tracking immune cells that dynamically change their shapes and motion patterns during wound healing in zebrafish.

Key outcomes include:
- An automated computational workflow for macrophage segmentation and tracking in 2D+time microscopy data
- Extension to 3D+time image segmentation for zebrafish morphogenesis data
- Quantitative analysis of macrophage morphology and behavioral transitions
